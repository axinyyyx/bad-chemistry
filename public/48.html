Cadmium

Cadmium is a soft, bluish-white metal that is toxic and used in batteries, coatings, and pigments.

Atomic Structure

Atomic Information

Property Value
Atomic Number 48
Symbol Cd
Atomic Mass 112.41 u
Electron Configuration 1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d10 4s2 4d10 5s2
State at Room Temperature Solid

Uses of Cadmium

  • Used in rechargeable batteries, especially in nickel-cadmium (NiCd) batteries.
  • Used as a protective coating for steel and in the production of pigments.
  • Used in electroplating and in certain alloys.
